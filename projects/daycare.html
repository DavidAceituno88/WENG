Post Frame Building for the YMCA Liverpool N.S

Published on December 2024 by Well Engineered

Project Overview

Well Engineered is offering design/build services for post-framed wooden buildings with spans up to 80 feet, ceiling heights up to 30 feet, and located near to the HRM. This is a versatile and cost-effective building method that, unlike traditional methods, uses fewer materials resulting in lower construction time and lower overall cost. In post-frame construction glulam (glued and laminated) posts are attached with moment connections to a slab, strip footing, or concrete pier. The posts are evenly spaced 8 to 12 ft apart with larger clear spans accommodated by carrying a beam spanning two post positions. A post pair, consisting of two posts on opposite walls, supports a roof truss. The post/truss assembly is connected to its neighbour with girts and purlins consisting of Simpson brand brackets and dimensional lumber. Because the posts provide lateral stability, very little temporary bracing is needed during construction, speeding assembly and reducing the risk of wind damage to the framing.

Lower carbon footprint

Post-framed buildings carry a far lower carbon footprint than their steel or concrete counterparts. This framing system has no load-bearing walls, similar to a steel building, but with better thermal performance and much easier modification and maintenance. Steel cladding is typically installed, both inside and outside for warehouse or service areas. With a steel roofing system, the building envelope can be entirely non-combustible. For interior aesthetics, the heavy wood posts can be partially exposed in the wall system, while maintaining the required fire resistance rating.

No load-bearing walls & Open spacious interiors

The inherent flexibility of post-frame design allows for spacious interiors without the need for interior support walls or columns. Clear spans of up to 80 feet can be accommodated. Additionally, the high ceilings allow for building mechanical rooms or mezzanines above offices or bathrooms. Post-frame construction generally has a shorter construction timeline and lower material costs than traditional concrete or steel buildings.

Low energy consumption

When used in conjunction with insulated sheathing systems, the post and girt wall system has essentially no thermal bridges resulting in a very efficient building envelope and low energy consumption. Metal roofing can be installed directly on the roof purlins eliminating the need for a sheathing system saving both time and money, and simplifying maintenance in the future. Metal roofing is generally resistant to water ingress, but any leakage that may occur over the coming decades can easily be detected and corrected in the attic space.

Overhangs in Building Design: Versatile Solutions for Weather Protection and Future Expansion

To shelter people or equipment from the weather, overhangs can be incorporated into either the gable or eave sides of the building. The posts, trusses, and purlins are simply installed as normal, however, no girts or wall framing is installed between the posts. This has the additional advantage that it can easily be closed in later in the life of the building creating additional interior space.
